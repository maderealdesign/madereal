# Web Design Prices in Lancashire

If you run a small business in Lancashire, website prices can feel confusing.

One person offers a cheap monthly deal. Another agency wants thousands. A DIY builder looks low-cost, but then you have to write the pages, choose the layout and work out what Google needs.

The right answer depends on what stage your business is at.

## What are you actually buying?

A useful local business website is not just a homepage. It should help people understand what you do, trust you, and contact you without friction.

For most small businesses in Colne, Burnley, Nelson, Pendle and nearby towns, a good starter website should include:

- a clear home page
- service pages
- a contact or booking form
- click-to-call and enquiry options
- mobile-first design
- local SEO basics
- location pages for nearby towns
- a clear ownership agreement

That is the practical starting point. Anything beyond that should have a clear reason.

## Common pricing routes

Most Lancashire businesses will see one of four options.

DIY builders are usually the cheapest monthly route, but you do the work yourself. They can be fine if you have time and confidence, but many business owners never finish the site properly.

Pay-monthly website packages can look easy because the upfront fee is low. The risk is long-term cost and ownership. If you pay every month just to keep a simple site online, the total can quickly overtake a one-off build.

Traditional freelancers and agencies can be a good fit when you need a deeper brand, custom functionality, ecommerce, booking systems or ongoing marketing. For a straightforward local service website, they can also be more than you need at the start.
