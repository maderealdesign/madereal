# What Makes a Website Look Professional?

A professional website is not defined by animations or expensive features. It is defined by whether customers trust it.

For small businesses, that trust usually comes from clarity, consistency, and proof.

## Clear visual hierarchy

Visitors should know where to look first. Strong headings, clean sections, readable text, and clear buttons make a website feel more confident.

If everything competes for attention, the site feels messy.

## Consistent design

Colours, fonts, spacing, buttons, icons, and images should feel like one system. This is where [graphic design](/graphic-design.html) and web design overlap.

A consistent brand makes the business feel more established.

## Good mobile layout

Professional on desktop but awkward on mobile is not good enough. Most local customers check businesses on phones.

Your site should be easy to read, tap, and navigate.

## Trust signals

Use reviews, photos, qualifications, awards, guarantees, address details, and real examples.

Different businesses need different trust signals. [Electrician websites](/services/tradesman-electricians.html) need qualifications. [Hospitality websites](/services/hospitality-websites.html) need menus and photos. [Professional services websites](/services/professional-services.html) need expertise and process.

## Clear calls to action

The visitor should always know what to do next. Call, book, enquire, order, visit, or [get a free preview](/get-started.html).
