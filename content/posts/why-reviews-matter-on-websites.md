# Why Reviews Matter on Websites

Reviews are not just nice extras. They are proof.

When a customer lands on your website, they are deciding whether your business feels safe enough to contact. Reviews help answer that question.

## Reviews reduce risk

Most customers worry about making the wrong choice. Will the business turn up? Is the food good? Is the service reliable? Is the work professional?

Reviews help reduce that uncertainty.

That is why they matter on [tradesman websites](/services/tradesman-websites.html), [hospitality websites](/services/hospitality-websites.html), [professional services websites](/services/professional-services.html), and [small business websites](/services/small-business-websites.html).

## Put reviews near decisions

Do not hide reviews on a separate page only. Place strong reviews near calls to action, service sections, quote forms, booking buttons, and contact areas.

If someone is deciding whether to call, a relevant review can help tip the decision.

## Use specific reviews

Specific reviews are more persuasive than generic praise.

For example, “came out the same day and fixed the leak” is stronger for a [roofing website](/services/tradesman-roofing.html) than “great service.”

## Connect reviews with Google

Your Google Business Profile reviews support local trust. Your website can reference reviews, show rating signals, and encourage customers to check your profile.

## Keep asking for them

After a good job, ask. Make it easy. The more real reviews you collect, the stronger your website and local presence become.
