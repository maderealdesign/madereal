# Google Business Profile and Your Website

Your Google Business Profile and your website should work as a pair.

The Google profile helps customers find you in Maps and local search. Your website gives them the detail and confidence they need before taking action.

## Why your Google profile is not enough

A Google profile is powerful, but it has limits. You cannot fully control the layout, service explanations, sales message, internal links, or how your offer is framed.

That is where your website matters.

For example, a roofer can use their profile for reviews and photos, but their [roofing website](/services/tradesman-roofing.html) can explain emergency repairs, flat roofs, guttering, guarantees, and quote requests in a much clearer way.

## What your website should add

Your website should support your Google profile with:

- Clear service descriptions
- Local area pages
- Reviews and proof
- Photos and project examples
- Click-to-call buttons
- Contact forms
- Strong page titles and meta descriptions

## Keep details consistent

Your business name, phone number, address, opening hours, and website link should match everywhere.

Google and customers both prefer consistency.

## Link both ways

Add your website to your Google profile. On your website, use your reviews, opening hours, service areas, and contact details clearly.

This creates a stronger trust loop.
