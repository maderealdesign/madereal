# How to Get More Local Enquiries

More website traffic is nice, but enquiries are the real goal.

If people visit your site and leave without calling, booking, or sending a message, something is getting in the way.

## Make the offer obvious

Your page should say what you do, where you work, and who you help. Do not make people decode it.

A [small business website](/services/small-business-websites.html) should be direct.

## Add trust before the button

People need a reason to act. Put reviews, photos, qualifications, guarantees, or examples near enquiry buttons.

For [roofing websites](/services/tradesman-roofing.html), proof of workmanship matters. For [professional services websites](/services/professional-services.html), expertise and process matter. For [hospitality websites](/services/hospitality-websites.html), photos, menus, and opening hours matter.

## Use local language

Mention the towns and areas you serve. Link to relevant local pages such as [web design Burnley](/web-design-burnley.html), [web design Colne](/web-design-colne.html), and [web design Pendle](/web-design-pendle.html).

## Make contact easy

Use clear buttons:

- Call now
- Request a quote
- Book a table
- Get a preview
- Send an enquiry

Do not rely on one tiny contact link in the header.

## Improve one page at a time

Start with your homepage or main service page. Make it clearer, faster, and more trustworthy. Then build supporting blog posts and location pages around it.
