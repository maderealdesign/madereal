# Flyer Design Tips for Local Businesses

Flyers still work when they are clear, targeted, and easy to act on.

The mistake is treating a flyer like a tiny poster packed with everything the business has ever done. A good flyer should make one offer obvious.

## Start with the action

Before designing anything, decide what you want someone to do:

- Call for a quote
- Visit your website
- Book a table
- Use a discount code
- Scan a QR code
- Visit your shop

The design should support that one action.

## Use a strong headline

Your headline should say the value quickly. “New cafe open in Colne” is clearer than “Welcome to our journey.”

Clear wins.

## Include trust signals

Add reviews, years of experience, location, guarantees, or photos where useful. For trades, a review and phone number can be more persuasive than a long paragraph.

## Connect print to your website

Your flyer should not exist alone. Add a short URL or QR code that sends people to a relevant page, such as [small business websites](/services/small-business-websites.html), [hospitality websites](/services/hospitality-websites.html), or your [free website preview](/get-started.html) page if you are promoting a service.

## Keep it readable

Use strong spacing, clear sections, and simple hierarchy. If everything shouts, nothing stands out.
