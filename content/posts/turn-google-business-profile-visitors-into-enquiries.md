# How to Turn Google Business Profile Visitors Into Website Enquiries

A Google Business Profile can get you seen. That does not automatically mean it gets you enquiries.

People may view your profile, check your photos, skim your reviews, tap your website, or call when you are busy on another job.

The job of your website is to catch that attention and turn it into a next step.

## Know what Google is showing you

Google Business Profile performance can show how people interact with your business on Search and Maps. That includes actions such as website clicks, calls and direction requests.

Those are useful signals.

If profile views are going up but enquiries are not, the issue may not be visibility. It may be trust, clarity or the next step.

That is why MadeReal tracks Google Business Profile actions alongside Search Console, GA4 events and Netlify form enquiries in the local monitoring dashboard.

## Do not rely only on calls

Calls are useful, but they are fragile.

If you are fitting a roof, rewiring a house, serving customers, working with a client or driving between jobs, you can miss the call. Some customers will not leave a voicemail. They will go back to Google and choose someone else.

Your website should give them another route:

- a simple quote form
- WhatsApp if you use it
- email if that suits your business
- a booking link if relevant
- a one-field enquiry form for quick follow-up

For MadeReal, that means the main funnel is not "hope the phone rings at the right time". It is "leave your mobile or email and we will send a free preview".

## Make the website match the profile

The website should feel like a natural next step from the profile.

Keep these details consistent:

- business name
- phone number
- address or service area
- opening hours
- services
- photos
- reviews and trust signals

If your profile says you cover Burnley, Colne, Nelson and Pendle, the website should support that with clear local pages such as [web design Burnley](/web-design-burnley.html), [web design Colne](/web-design-colne.html), [web design Nelson](/web-design-nelson.html), and [web design Pendle](/web-design-pendle.html).

The same applies to your own business. Customers should not feel a mismatch after clicking through from Google.

## Build pages around customer questions

People click from a profile because they want detail.

Your website should answer:

- What do you do?
- Where do you work?
- Can I trust you?
- What does it cost?
- What happens next?
- How do I enquire without waiting on hold?

That is why a [small business website](/services/small-business-websites.html) should include more than a logo and contact page.

For trades, it may need proof and quote routes. For cafes and restaurants, it may need menus, opening times and booking details. For professional services, it may need trust, process and clear service pages.

## Use your website for the details Google cannot hold

Your Google profile is not designed to explain everything.

Your website can give space to:

- full service descriptions
- location landing pages
- before and after photos
- case studies
- FAQs
- pricing explanations
- quote forms
- Google review highlights

MadeReal's [website examples](/case-studies.html) show how proof and service details can support enquiries.

## What to monitor each week

Do not guess. Track the signals.

Useful weekly checks include:

- Search Console clicks and impressions
- Google Business Profile website clicks
- Google Business Profile calls
- direction requests if location visits matter
- GA4 lead events
- Netlify form submissions
- which pages have enquiry forms

MadeReal's monitoring workflow does this in `seo/dashboard.md` and the weekly review notes.

If Google profile actions go up but form submissions do not, improve the landing page. If form submissions go up but sales do not, improve the follow-up.

## Generative search is not a shortcut

Google's own guidance for AI features still points back to normal SEO foundations: useful content, crawlable pages, technical eligibility, clear business details and helpful local information.

For local businesses, a complete Google Business Profile and a useful website still matter.

That means the practical work is not an exploit. It is making the business easier to understand, trust and contact.
