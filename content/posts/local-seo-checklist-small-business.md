# Local SEO Checklist for Small Businesses

Local SEO is about helping nearby customers find and trust your business. It is not magic. It is clarity, consistency, useful content, and a website that supports your Google Business Profile.

## 1. Make your service obvious

Your website should clearly say what you do in plain language. “Creative solutions” is vague. “Affordable web design for small businesses in Lancashire” is clear.

That is why MadeReal has focused service pages for [small business websites](/services/small-business-websites.html), [hospitality websites](/services/hospitality-websites.html), [professional services websites](/services/professional-services.html), and [tradesman websites](/services/tradesman-websites.html).

## 2. Mention your real locations

If you serve Burnley, Colne, Nelson, Pendle, Blackburn, Skipton, or Clitheroe, your site should say so naturally. Location pages like [web design Burnley](/web-design-burnley.html), [web design Colne](/web-design-colne.html), and [web design Blackburn](/web-design-blackburn.html) give Google clearer local context.

## 3. Keep your contact details consistent

Your name, address, phone number, opening hours, and website should match across your website, Google profile, social profiles, and directories.

Inconsistent details create doubt.

## 4. Use reviews properly

Reviews are not just decoration. Place them near calls to action and service sections. Customers want proof before they call.

## 5. Build useful internal links

Every important page should connect to related pages. Blog posts should link to service pages, location pages, and the [free website preview](/get-started.html).

## 6. Make the site fast on mobile

Most local searches happen on phones. If your site is slow, cluttered, or hard to tap, you lose enquiries.
