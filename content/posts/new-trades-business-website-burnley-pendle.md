# Best Website Setup for a New Trades Business in Burnley or Pendle

When you start a trade business, the website does not need to be complicated.

It does need to make you look real, trustworthy and easy to contact. That matters even more if you are new, because customers do not yet know your name.

For trades around Burnley, Pendle, Colne, Nelson and nearby Lancashire towns, the first website should be built around enquiries.

## Start with the jobs you actually want

Do not list every possible job if you only want certain work.

A new trades business should make the main services obvious:

- emergency repairs
- installations
- maintenance
- inspections
- upgrades
- quote visits
- specific jobs you want more of

If you are a roofer, that might mean leak repairs, flat roofs, gutters and fascias. If you are an electrician, it might mean rewires, consumer units, inspections and emergency callouts.

## Make the local area clear

Customers often search by town. They do not just search "joiner" or "roofer". They search with places attached, such as Burnley, Pendle, Colne, Nelson or Blackburn.

Your site should mention the areas you genuinely cover. It should also make sense to humans, not just Google.

## Use proof, even if you are new

You may not have hundreds of reviews yet. That is fine.

Start with the proof you do have:

- photos of real work
- a short story about your experience
- qualifications
- insurance details
- clear contact details
- before and after photos
- supplier or manufacturer credentials
- early customer testimonials

Customers need reassurance before they invite someone into their home or business. A simple proof section can do a lot of work.

## Add a quote form and click-to-call

A trade website should not make people hunt for the next step.

At minimum, give people:

- click-to-call on mobile
- a simple quote form
- WhatsApp or email if you use them
- a way to describe the job
- a town or postcode field

Some people want to call. Some do not. If you miss calls during the working day, the form matters because it catches the enquiry while you are on another job.

## Keep the first build focused

For a new trades business, a sensible starter website usually includes:

- home page
- services page
- individual service sections
- about or trust section
- contact or quote page
- local area pages
- Google Business Profile support

You can add more later when you know which pages bring enquiries.
